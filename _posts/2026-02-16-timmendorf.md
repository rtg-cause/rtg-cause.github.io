<p><h2>One Year In</h2> </p>

We are happy to announce the conclusion of this year's academic retreat. Here, at Timmendorfer Strand, the whole research training group CAUSE came together to synchronize and present our past research, discuss and explore current developments in the field of explainable software, and discover new opportunities for future collaborations and research directions. 
The retreat featured two keynote talks by experts in the fields of software verification and wind farm control. Prof. Dr. Christel Baier (University of Dresden) presented "Verification meets Causality", a talk connecting the field of verification with reasoning about what influences the results. Dr. Vlaho Petrovic presented "Wind Park Control", a talk about the physical and technical challenges in the control of wind parks and individual wind engines. In addition, the members of CAUSE organized presentations and posters about their own projects. 
Carrying the inspiration from the presentations and conversations in the breakroom, the PhD students and PIs split into groups to discuss implications and ideas. These workshops sparked multiple projects and paper concepts to work on and expand on thoughts gathered during the retreat. 
The reserved rooms provided productive environments and allowed for focused work sessions, while the central break room invited discussion and energy recharge. Walks by the seaside in the mornings and evenings kept the mind sharp even with a full program during the day. We are looking forward to the future work branching off from this successful retreat at Timmendorfer Strand.
